## FIR-003-ELEVATOR-CONFINED - Lift Entrapment / Confined Person Without Immediate Medical Threat

**Category:** Fire/Rescue/Medical if needed  
**Priority:** P3 unless medical emergency, heat, smoke, panic attack, child/elderly/high-risk  
**Source basis:** Rescue safety and call management principles  
**Retrieval tags:** stuck in lift, elevator trapped, cannot open door, panic in lift

### Dispatcher objective
Prevent self-rescue injury, dispatch building maintenance/fire rescue as needed, monitor for medical deterioration.

### Critical questions
- Exact building/lift number/floor?
- How many people?
- Any medical emergency, breathing difficulty, pregnancy, child or elderly?
- Any smoke/fire/water?
- Is the lift moving or unstable?
- Can you contact building security?

### Caller response script
Say: "Do not try to force the doors or climb out. Stay inside unless responders tell you otherwise." Ask them to press alarm/intercom if not already done. Keep them calm: "Help is being arranged. Sit down if you feel dizzy. Tell me immediately if anyone has chest pain, breathing difficulty or faints."

### Responder guidance
Notify building management and Bomba/rescue according to local policy. Add ambulance if medical symptoms, vulnerable person or prolonged entrapment. Handoff: building, lift number/floor, number trapped, medical status, alarm/intercom, heat/ventilation and access contacts.

### Do not
Do not tell caller to climb out between floors. Do not leave medical symptoms untriaged.

### RAG retrieval note
Retrieve this SOP when the transcript or classifier detects the trigger phrases above, a matching chief complaint, or a priority-changing life threat. If multiple protocols match, prefer the one with the immediate life threat and attach secondary SOPs as supporting context.
