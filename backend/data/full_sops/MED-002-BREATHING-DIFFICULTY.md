## MED-002-BREATHING-DIFFICULTY - Severe Breathing Difficulty / Asthma / Respiratory Distress

**Category:** Medical  
**Priority:** P2_EMERGENCY or P1 if unable to speak/turning blue/not breathing  
**Source basis:** IFRC breathing problems, MOH emergency care framing  
**Retrieval tags:** short of breath, asthma attack, cannot breathe, wheezing, blue, choking sensation, allergic reaction

### Dispatcher objective
Identify life-threatening breathing difficulty, keep patient positioned for breathing, dispatch medical response and escalate if consciousness/breathing worsens.

### Critical questions
- Exact location?
- Is the patient awake?
- Can they speak full sentences?
- Are lips or face blue/grey?
- Is there chest pain?
- Any known asthma/allergy?
- Do they have prescribed inhaler/medication?
- Any choking or foreign object?

### Caller response script
Say: "Help is being arranged. Keep the person sitting upright if that helps them breathe. Do not force them to lie down." Ask whether they can speak. If the patient cannot speak, is turning blue/grey, becoming drowsy, or breathing stops, upgrade to P1 and retrieve cardiac arrest/choking protocol as relevant.

If the patient has a prescribed inhaler or emergency medication available: "Help them use their own prescribed medicine as they normally do. Do not give someone else's medicine." If the patient has anaphylaxis signs such as swelling of face/tongue, widespread rash and breathing trouble, escalate as severe allergic reaction.

Keep the environment calm: loosen tight clothing if safe, move away from smoke/fumes, and keep crowds back. Tell caller: "Watch their breathing. Tell me immediately if they become drowsy, collapse, or stop breathing normally."

### Responder guidance
Send ambulance. Include ability to speak, colour, consciousness, suspected asthma/allergy/smoke inhalation, medication used, time of onset and environmental hazards. If smoke, chemical or gas exposure is involved, also notify fire/hazmat and stage responders until scene safety is confirmed.

### Do not
Do not tell caller to give unprescribed medicine. Do not ignore breathing difficulty because the patient is young. Do not keep patient walking around.
