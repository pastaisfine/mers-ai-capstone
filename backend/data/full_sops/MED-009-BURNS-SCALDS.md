## MED-009-BURNS-SCALDS - Burns / Scalds / Person on Fire

**Category:** Medical/Fire  
**Priority:** P1 if airway/fire/large burn; P2 for serious burns  
**Source basis:** IFRC first aid, UTM fire SOP, Bomba/fire response framing  
**Retrieval tags:** burn, scald, on fire, explosion, chemical burn, electrical burn, smoke inhalation

### Dispatcher objective
Stop burning process, cool thermal burns when safe, identify airway/chemical/electrical danger and dispatch appropriate responders.

### Critical questions
- Exact location?
- Is the person still on fire or in smoke?
- Are they awake and breathing?
- What caused the burn?
- Where and how large is the burn?
- Any face/neck burn or breathing trouble?
- Chemical/electrical involved?

### Caller response script
If person is on fire: "Stop them moving. Get them down and roll them, or cover flames with a heavy cloth if safe." Move away from smoke/fire. For thermal burn after safety: "Cool the burn with clean running water if available. Do not apply oil, butter, toothpaste or creams. Remove jewellery or tight items near the burn if easy, but do not pull off stuck clothing."

For chemical burns, retrieve chemical exposure protocol. For electrical injury, ensure power is off before touching patient. If breathing difficulty or unconsciousness occurs, switch to airway/cardiac arrest protocol.

### Responder guidance
Ambulance for significant burns; add Bomba for active fire, explosion, chemical/electrical hazard, trapped persons or smoke. Handoff: burn cause, location/extent, face/airway involvement, breathing, chemical/electrical status, cooling started and hazards.

### Do not
Do not advise applying household remedies. Do not touch electrical victim until power isolated. Do not delay evacuation from smoke.
