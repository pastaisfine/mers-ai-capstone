## DIS-001-FLOOD-LANDSLIDE - Flood / Landslide / Severe Weather Rescue

**Category:** Disaster/Rescue/Medical/Police  
**Priority:** P1 if trapped/in water/injury; MCI if widespread  
**Source basis:** NADMA National Risk Register, flood/disaster sources in seed list  
**Retrieval tags:** flood, flash flood, landslide, water rising, trapped on roof, mudslide, evacuation

### Dispatcher objective
Move caller away from immediate danger, identify rescue location, coordinate multi-agency response and disaster escalation.

### Critical questions
- Exact location/GPS/landmark?
- How many people and ages?
- Water depth and rising/falling?
- Are people in water or trapped on roof/car?
- Any injuries, elderly, children, disabled, pregnant?
- Electricity/gas hazards?
- Safe access route?

### Caller response script
Say: "Move to higher ground if you can do so safely. Do not walk or drive through moving floodwater." If trapped: "Stay where rescuers can see you if it is safe. Keep phone battery. Signal with light or bright cloth if available." For landslide: "Move away from slope, fallen trees, cracks and unstable ground. Do not go back to retrieve belongings."

Ask caller to group people together, count vulnerable persons and conserve battery while staying reachable.

### Responder guidance
Dispatch rescue/Bomba, police for traffic/evacuation and ambulance for injuries/vulnerable persons. Escalate to disaster/MCI if multiple locations or resource saturation. Handoff: exact coordinates/landmarks, number of persons, vulnerable groups, water depth/current, landslide stability, access route and staging area.

### Do not
Do not instruct callers to enter moving water. Do not use road names alone in floods if landmarks/GPS are available.
