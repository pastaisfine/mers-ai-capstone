## TRA-002-FALL-HEAD-INJURY - Fall / Head Injury / Suspected Spinal Injury

**Category:** Medical/Trauma  
**Priority:** P1 if unconscious/not breathing/severe bleeding; P2 if head injury, elderly, high fall or neurological symptoms  
**Source basis:** TLSM trauma, MOH head injury sources in seed list  
**Retrieval tags:** fell, hit head, unconscious after fall, neck pain, cannot move, elderly fall, bleeding head

### Dispatcher objective
Identify life threats and neurological risk, prevent unnecessary movement, dispatch appropriate medical response.

### Critical questions
- Exact location?
- How far did they fall?
- Are they awake?
- Are they breathing normally?
- Any neck/back pain?
- Any weakness, numbness, seizure, vomiting or confusion?
- Any blood thinner medication?
- Any bleeding?

### Caller response script
Say: "Help is being arranged. Do not move the person unless they are in immediate danger." If conscious: "Tell them to stay still. Keep their head and neck as still as possible. Do not give food or drink." If bleeding, use direct pressure around the wound while avoiding pressure on suspected skull fracture if obvious severe head trauma; local SOP should specify exact wording.

If unconscious but breathing: keep airway safe according to approved dispatcher instruction and monitor breathing. If not breathing normally, switch to CPR protocol.

### Responder guidance
Ambulance. Include fall height/mechanism, age, consciousness, vomiting/seizure/confusion, neck/back pain, anticoagulants, bleeding and access challenges. If confined space, construction site or height rescue is involved, add Bomba/rescue.

### Do not
Do not tell caller to sit/stand the patient up after head injury. Do not ignore elderly falls. Do not assume alcohol/drugs explain altered mental status.
