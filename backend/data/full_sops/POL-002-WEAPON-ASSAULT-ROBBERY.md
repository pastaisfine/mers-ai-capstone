## POL-002-WEAPON-ASSAULT-ROBBERY - Weapon Threat / Assault / Robbery in Progress

**Category:** Police/Medical  
**Priority:** P1 if in progress/weapon/injury; P2 if suspect left but injuries/risk remain  
**Source basis:** Police dispatch safety principles, emergency dispatcher communication sources  
**Retrieval tags:** knife, gun, robbery, being attacked, fight, stabbed, armed, hostage

### Dispatcher objective
Keep caller hidden/safe, gather suspect and scene details without increasing risk, dispatch police and medical as needed.

### Critical questions
- Exact location?
- Are you safe to talk?
- Is the suspect still there?
- What weapon?
- Any injuries?
- How many suspects?
- Description/direction of travel?
- Any hostages or children?

### Caller response script
Say softly if needed: "Get to safety if you can. Do not confront them. Stay hidden and quiet if that is safest." If caller is witnessing from safety: "Stay where you are safe. Do not follow the suspect. Tell me what you can see from where you are."

If injured person is with caller and scene is safe, retrieve bleeding/trauma protocol. If scene is unsafe, prioritize safety and police response first.

### Responder guidance
Police response; ambulance staged until scene secure if violence ongoing. Handoff: suspect present/left, weapon type, number of suspects, descriptions, direction of travel, injuries, hostages, caller location and safe approach/staging.

### Do not
Do not instruct caller to chase, film up close, disarm or confront suspect. Do not broadcast caller identity in a way that could endanger them.

### RAG retrieval note
Retrieve this SOP when the transcript or classifier detects the trigger phrases above, a matching chief complaint, or a priority-changing life threat. If multiple protocols match, prefer the one with the immediate life threat and attach secondary SOPs as supporting context.
