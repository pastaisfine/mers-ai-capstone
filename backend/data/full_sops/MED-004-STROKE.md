## MED-004-STROKE - Suspected Stroke / FAST Positive

**Category:** Medical  
**Priority:** P2_EMERGENCY; P1 if unconscious/not breathing  
**Source basis:** Malaysia Ischaemic Stroke CPG, MOH emergency care framing  
**Retrieval tags:** face droop, slurred speech, weak arm, sudden weakness, sudden confusion, stroke, cannot talk, one side numb

### Dispatcher objective
Identify stroke signs, record last-known-well time, dispatch time-sensitive ambulance and notify stroke-capable pathway where available.

### Critical questions
- Exact location?
- Is the patient awake and breathing normally?
- When was the patient last seen normal?
- Is the face uneven when smiling?
- Can they lift both arms?
- Is speech slurred or strange?
- Any seizure, fall or head injury?
- What is the patient's age?

### Caller response script
Say: "This may be time-sensitive. Help is being arranged. Keep the patient resting and do not give food or drink." Ask for last-known-well time exactly: "When was the last time someone saw them normal?" If unknown, record "unknown" and ask who may know.

Tell caller: "Keep them safe and comfortable. If they vomit or become very drowsy, tell me immediately. If they stop breathing normally, I will guide you." Do not ask the caller to drive the patient unless instructed by local dispatch policy; ambulance routing allows pre-notification and safer care.

### Responder guidance
Send ambulance and flag suspected stroke. Include FAST findings, last-known-well time, anticoagulant history if known, seizure/fall/head injury, baseline disability if known and preferred stroke-capable destination according to local protocol.

### Do not
Do not reassure that symptoms will pass. Do not give food, drink or medication instructions beyond approved local SOP. Do not miss last-known-well time.

### RAG retrieval note
Retrieve this SOP when the transcript or classifier detects the trigger phrases above, a matching chief complaint, or a priority-changing life threat. If multiple protocols match, prefer the one with the immediate life threat and attach secondary SOPs as supporting context.
