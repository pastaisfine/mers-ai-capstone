## MED-011-DROWNING - Drowning / Near Drowning / Water Rescue

**Category:** Medical/Rescue  
**Priority:** P1  
**Source basis:** IFRC first aid, rescue safety principles, NADMA multi-agency response  
**Retrieval tags:** drowning, fell into water, not breathing after swimming, river rescue, flood water

### Dispatcher objective
Protect rescuers, coordinate rescue/ambulance, start CPR when safe and victim is out of water and not breathing normally.

### Critical questions
- Exact location/water body?
- Is the person still in the water?
- Can they be reached safely without entering?
- How long underwater?
- Are they awake/breathing?
- Any flood/current/electrical hazard?
- How many people involved?

### Caller response script
Say: "Do not enter dangerous water if you are not trained. Call out to them. Reach or throw something floating if safe." If the person is out of water: "Check if they are awake and breathing normally." If not breathing normally, retrieve CPR protocol. If breathing, keep them warm and monitor closely.

If flood or fast current is present: "Move to higher ground if you can. Stay away from drains, rivers and electricity. Tell me your safest visible landmark."

### Responder guidance
Dispatch ambulance and water rescue/Bomba as appropriate. Handoff: exact water location, access point, number in water, last seen point, current/flood condition, victim out/not out, breathing status, CPR started and hazards.

### Do not
Do not direct untrained callers into water. Do not delay CPR once victim is safely out and not breathing normally.
