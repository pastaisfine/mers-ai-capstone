## MED-001-CARDIAC-ARREST - Unconscious Person / Not Breathing / Suspected Cardiac Arrest

**Category:** Medical  
**Priority:** P1_IMMEDIATE_LIFE_THREAT  
**Source basis:** AHA T-CPR, Red Cross dispatcher-assisted CPR, IFRC first aid, MOH emergency care framing  
**Retrieval tags:** collapsed, unconscious, not breathing, breathing strangely, gasping, blue lips, no response

### Dispatcher objective

Identify cardiac arrest quickly, dispatch highest medical response, guide caller through compression-only CPR when untrained, and keep updates flowing to responders.

### Critical questions

- What is the exact location?
- Is the patient awake?
- Is the patient breathing normally?
- Can you put the phone on speaker?
- Is there an AED nearby?
- Is anyone else there to help?
- Is the scene safe?

### Caller response script

Say: "I am sending help. Stay on the line. Put the phone on speaker if you can." Ask: "Is he awake?" If no, ask: "Is he breathing normally - yes or no?" If the caller says no, unsure, only gasping, or breathing strangely, treat as suspected cardiac arrest.

Instruction for untrained caller: "Lay him flat on his back on the floor. Kneel beside his chest. Put the heel of one hand in the centre of the chest, put your other hand on top. Keep your arms straight. Push hard and fast. Let the chest come back up each time. I will count with you. Do not stop unless he wakes up, breathes normally, an AED is ready, or responders take over."

If an AED is available: "Send someone to get the AED now. Keep doing compressions until it arrives. When it arrives, turn it on and follow the voice instructions." Do not delay compressions to search alone for an AED.

If caller is exhausted: "If another adult is there, change over quickly. Keep the gap as short as possible."

### Responder guidance

Recommend ALS ambulance/highest medical response. Handoff must include time collapse noticed, breathing status, CPR start time, AED availability, caller relationship, access route, floor/unit and known hazards. If caller reports trauma, electrocution, drowning, overdose or hanging, include mechanism because responders may need additional safety/clinical resources.

### Do not

Do not ask long medical history before CPR. Do not say the person is dead. Do not stop instructions because caller is crying. Do not claim CPR will guarantee survival.
