## MED-005-CHOKING - Choking / Airway Obstruction

**Category:** Medical  
**Priority:** P1 if cannot cough/speak/breathe; P2 if partially choking  
**Source basis:** IFRC first aid, Red Cross/AHA first aid principles  
**Retrieval tags:** choking, food stuck, cannot breathe, cannot speak, turning blue, hands on throat

### Dispatcher objective
Differentiate partial from severe airway obstruction, dispatch if severe and coach caller through safe first aid according to age/condition.

### Critical questions
- Exact location?
- Is the person coughing or able to speak?
- Are they breathing?
- Adult, child or infant?
- Are they conscious?
- What are they choking on?
- Any pregnancy or large body size considerations?

### Caller response script
If the person can cough, speak or breathe: "Encourage them to keep coughing. Do not put your fingers in their mouth. Watch closely. Tell me if they cannot breathe or speak."

If the person cannot breathe, cough effectively or speak, dispatch P1. For adult/child caller instruction, use the local approved choking script. A general safe script is: "Stand behind them if you can. Give firm blows between the shoulder blades. If that does not clear it, give abdominal thrusts if you know how and it is safe. Keep alternating as trained until the object comes out or they become unconscious." For infants, retrieve infant-specific choking protocol; do not use adult abdominal thrusts.

If the person becomes unconscious: "Lay them flat on the floor. I will guide CPR. If you see an object clearly in the mouth, remove it. Do not do blind finger sweeps."

### Responder guidance
Send ambulance for severe choking, unconsciousness, persistent breathing difficulty or after significant choking event. Include age, object, current breathing, consciousness, first aid attempted and whether object expelled. If restaurant/school/public venue, include access point and crowd conditions.

### Do not
Do not advise blind finger sweeps. Do not delay dispatch for severe choking. Do not use adult instructions for infants.
