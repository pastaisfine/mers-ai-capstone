## MH-001-SUICIDE-SELF-HARM - Suicidal Crisis / Self-Harm Risk

**Category:** Mental Health/Police/Medical  
**Priority:** P1 if active attempt/weapon/overdose/high-risk location; P2 otherwise urgent  
**Source basis:** Psychological first aid principles, emergency dispatcher communication, poisoning/trauma protocols as needed  
**Retrieval tags:** suicide, want to die, self harm, took pills, on bridge, cutting, hanging, jump

### Dispatcher objective
Keep the person engaged and alive, dispatch appropriate emergency response, reduce immediate access to means if safe and avoid judgement.

### Critical questions
- Exact location?
- Are you with the person or are you the person?
- Are they in immediate danger right now?
- Have they taken anything or injured themselves?
- Do they have a weapon or means with them?
- Are they alone?
- Can someone safe stay nearby?

### Caller response script
Use direct and compassionate language: "I'm glad you called. I will stay with you while help is arranged." Ask location early. If the caller is the person at risk: "Move away from the edge/weapon/medicine if you can. Put some distance between you and it. You do not have to solve everything right now; just stay with me for the next minute."

If a bystander is calling: "Do not argue, threaten, dare, shame or crowd them. Speak calmly. Keep them talking. If safe, remove other people from the area and wait for responders." If overdose/injury exists, retrieve poisoning/bleeding/CPR protocols.

### Responder guidance
Dispatch police/medical according to local mental health crisis SOP. Handoff: exact location, active attempt or ideation, means present, injuries/ingestion, person's name/age, alone or with caller, safe approach concerns and de-escalation notes. Consider ambulance and mental health crisis resources if available.

### Do not
Do not say they are selfish, illegal, attention-seeking or wasting resources. Do not provide details about self-harm methods. Do not disconnect while immediate risk remains unless safety requires it.

### RAG retrieval note
Retrieve this SOP when the transcript or classifier detects the trigger phrases above, a matching chief complaint, or a priority-changing life threat. If multiple protocols match, prefer the one with the immediate life threat and attach secondary SOPs as supporting context.
