## MED-010-CHILDBIRTH - Imminent Childbirth / Pregnancy Emergency

**Category:** Medical  
**Priority:** P2; P1 if severe bleeding, unconscious, seizure, baby not breathing or cord complications  
**Source basis:** IFRC first aid, emergency dispatch pre-arrival instruction concepts  
**Retrieval tags:** giving birth, baby coming, pregnant bleeding, labour, water broke, contractions

### Dispatcher objective
Dispatch ambulance, identify imminent delivery and complications, provide calm preparation and newborn safety instructions through approved script.

### Critical questions
- Exact location?
- How many weeks pregnant?
- Is the mother awake and breathing normally?
- Any heavy bleeding?
- Are contractions very close?
- Can she feel the baby coming or see the head?
- Any known complications or twins?

### Caller response script
Say: "Help is being arranged. Keep the mother as comfortable and private as possible." If delivery is not imminent, encourage rest on side or comfortable position according to local SOP, prepare towels and unlock door. If baby is coming now, retrieve approved childbirth pre-arrival script and escalate to human dispatcher.

After birth, general safe principle: keep baby warm and near mother, do not pull the cord, monitor baby's breathing/crying, and report heavy bleeding or baby not breathing immediately. If baby not breathing, retrieve newborn resuscitation script only if locally approved.

### Responder guidance
Ambulance. Include gestational age, contraction frequency, crowning/visible head, bleeding, mother's consciousness, baby delivered/not delivered, newborn breathing/crying status, complications and access notes.

### Do not
Do not tell caller to pull baby or cord. Do not give complex obstetric instructions without approved script. Do not ignore heavy bleeding.

### RAG retrieval note
Retrieve this SOP when the transcript or classifier detects the trigger phrases above, a matching chief complaint, or a priority-changing life threat. If multiple protocols match, prefer the one with the immediate life threat and attach secondary SOPs as supporting context.
