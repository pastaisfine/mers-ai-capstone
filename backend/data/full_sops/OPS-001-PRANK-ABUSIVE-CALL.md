## OPS-001-PRANK-ABUSIVE-CALL - Prank, Abusive or Non-Emergency Calls While Preserving Safety

**Category:** Call management/Operations  
**Priority:** P4 unless risk indicators emerge  
**Source basis:** Emergency dispatcher support guide, operational safety principles  
**Retrieval tags:** joking, abusive caller, repeated non-emergency, silent suspicious, hoax

### Dispatcher objective
Avoid resource misuse while not missing concealed emergencies. Maintain professionalism and document repeat abuse.

### Critical questions
- What is the emergency?
- Where is this happening?
- Are police, ambulance or fire needed?
- Is anyone in immediate danger?

### Caller response script
Use neutral limit setting: "999 is for emergencies. Tell me now if someone needs police, ambulance or fire." If abusive: "I can help if there is an emergency. I need the location and what happened." If no emergency after verification, follow local non-emergency referral/disconnection policy.

For suspected prank by child, verify calmly before ending: "Is anyone hurt? Is there fire? Are you safe? Can I speak to an adult?" Document repeat numbers according to policy.

### Responder guidance
If no emergency and policy allows, no dispatch or refer to non-emergency line. If repeated malicious calls or risk indicators, notify supervisor/police according to local policy. Preserve call metadata.

### Do not
Do not become angry or sarcastic. Do not assume prank when there are background signs of distress, child caller, domestic violence risk or silent-call clues.
