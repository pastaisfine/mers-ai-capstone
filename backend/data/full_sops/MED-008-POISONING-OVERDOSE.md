## MED-008-POISONING-OVERDOSE - Poisoning / Overdose / Toxic Ingestion

**Category:** Medical/Hazmat/Police as needed  
**Priority:** P1 if unconscious/not breathing; P2 otherwise urgent  
**Source basis:** IFRC first aid, MOH emergency care framing, hazmat source list  
**Retrieval tags:** overdose, poison, took pills, chemical swallowed, carbon monoxide, gas leak, pesticide

### Dispatcher objective
Protect caller, identify substance if safe, avoid unsafe home remedies, dispatch ambulance and hazmat/fire if environmental exposure.

### Critical questions
- Exact location?
- Is the patient awake and breathing normally?
- What substance and how much?
- When did exposure happen?
- Was it swallowed, inhaled, injected or on skin?
- Is the scene safe or are fumes present?
- How many people exposed?
- Any intentional self-harm?

### Caller response script
Say: "Help is being arranged. Do not taste, smell or touch the substance. Move away from fumes if it is safe." If inhalation/gas: "Get to fresh air now if you can do so safely. Do not switch electrical items on or off if there may be gas." If swallowed: "Do not make them vomit unless a poison specialist or approved local SOP says so. Keep the container or label for responders if safe."

If intentional overdose or self-harm is suspected, use supportive non-judgmental language: "You are not in trouble. We are sending help. Stay with me." Keep the person supervised if safe and remove immediate hazards only if it does not endanger caller.

### Responder guidance
Ambulance; add Bomba/hazmat for gas/chemical/fumes, police if intentional harm, violence or unsafe scene. Handoff: substance, dose/amount, time, route, symptoms, consciousness/breathing, number exposed, container availability and scene hazards.

### Do not
Do not recommend vomiting, food, drink or antidotes without approved medical control. Do not send unprotected responders into fumes.
