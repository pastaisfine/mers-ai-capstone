## SOP-001-CALL-CYCLE - Universal Emergency Call Cycle

**Tags:** call-taking, dispatcher workflow, case entry, key questions, pre-arrival instructions, post-dispatch

The universal call cycle should be followed for all emergency incidents. The flow is intentionally simple because emergency calls are noisy, stressful and time-sensitive.

1. Answer immediately and identify the emergency line: "999 emergency, what is your emergency?" If the system is localised, the Malay version may be: "Talian kecemasan 999, apakah kecemasan anda?"
2. Lock the location before long questioning. Ask for exact address, landmark, road direction, floor/unit number, gate code, nearby mosque/school/shop, GPS pin if available, and whether the scene is safe to approach. Repeat the location back.
3. Obtain callback number and caller relationship to victim. If the line drops, the system must preserve the last known location and call-back number.
4. Identify the chief complaint in plain language: medical, fire, police/security, road crash, disaster/rescue, hazardous material, domestic violence, mental health crisis or unknown.
5. Screen for immediate life threats: not breathing, unconscious, severe bleeding, trapped, fire/smoke, weapon, drowning, chemical exposure, multiple casualties, unsafe scene.
6. Dispatch or recommend early dispatch once a high-probability P1/P2 threat is detected. Do not wait for every detail before dispatching if life threat is clear.
7. Continue key questions while response is being arranged: age, sex, number of patients, symptoms, mechanism, hazards, access route and special needs.
8. Provide pre-arrival instructions only from retrieved SOP. Give one action at a time. Confirm completion before moving on.
9. Keep the caller on the line when useful and safe. If caller must move to safety, tell them to move first and take the phone if possible.
10. Update responders through CAD/handoff notes whenever new facts emerge.
11. Close only when safe: caller no longer needs instructions, responders have arrived, or a human dispatcher takes over.

The AI agent should never conduct the conversation as a casual chatbot. It should behave like a structured call-taking assistant: calm, precise, time-aware and always oriented toward dispatch readiness.
