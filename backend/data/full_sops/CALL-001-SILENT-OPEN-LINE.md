## CALL-001-SILENT-OPEN-LINE - Silent Call / Open Line / Caller Cannot Speak

**Category:** Call management/Police/Medical/Fire as indicated  
**Priority:** Risk-based; escalate if distress/noise/location risk  
**Source basis:** Emergency dispatch communication principles  
**Retrieval tags:** silent, open line, no speech, background screaming, caller whispering, hang up

### Dispatcher objective
Preserve location, infer danger from audio/context, use low-risk yes/no prompts, call back if safe and escalate when threat is plausible.

### Critical questions
- Can you speak safely?
- Press any key or make a sound if you need help.
- Is someone preventing you from speaking?
- Do you need police? ambulance? fire?

### Caller response script
Say calmly: "This is emergency 999. If you cannot speak, stay on the line." If safe, ask: "Tap once for yes, twice for no." Use yes/no questions: "Do you need police?" "Do you need ambulance?" "Are you in danger now?" Keep voice neutral if caller may be overheard.

If call drops, follow local callback policy. If background indicates violence, distress, fire alarms, crash sounds or medical distress, escalate for welfare/police/medical response based on available location and risk.

### Responder guidance
Handoff must include phone number, approximate location/GPS/cell data, background sounds, whether caller could answer, callback attempts and suspected incident type. For domestic violence/hostage risk, request discreet approach.

### Do not
Do not assume prank because caller is silent. Do not reveal sensitive assumptions aloud if caller may be overheard.
