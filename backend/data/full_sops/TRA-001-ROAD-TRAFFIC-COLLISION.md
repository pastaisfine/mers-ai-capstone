## TRA-001-ROAD-TRAFFIC-COLLISION - Road Traffic Collision / Motor Vehicle Crash

**Category:** Medical/Rescue/Police  
**Priority:** P1/P2 depending on injuries, entrapment and hazards  
**Source basis:** TLSM trauma, field triage principles, NADMA multi-agency coordination  
**Retrieval tags:** car accident, motorbike crash, lorry collision, hit pedestrian, trapped, rollover, bleeding roadside

### Dispatcher objective
Secure location, identify hazards, classify casualty severity, coordinate ambulance, police and fire/rescue as needed.

### Critical questions
- Exact road name and direction?
- Nearest kilometre marker/exit/landmark?
- How many vehicles?
- How many injured?
- Anyone trapped?
- Any fire, fuel leak, smoke, power lines or hazardous cargo?
- Is traffic still moving around them?
- Are patients awake and breathing?

### Caller response script
Say: "Do not put yourself in traffic. Move to a safe place if you can." If the caller is a bystander, instruct them not to stand in the roadway. Ask them to turn on hazard lights only if safe. Tell them not to move injured persons unless there is immediate danger such as fire, smoke, drowning or traffic impact.

For a conscious injured person: "Tell them to stay still. Support their head only if you can do so safely. Keep them warm. Do not give food or drink." For bleeding, retrieve severe bleeding protocol. If not breathing, retrieve cardiac arrest protocol.

### Responder guidance
Dispatch ambulance and police; add Bomba/rescue for entrapment, fire, fuel leak, hazardous material, heavy vehicle or difficult extrication. Handoff: road direction, lane blocked, patient count, trapped status, fire/fuel/electrical hazards, vehicle types, severe injuries, caller safety and best access route.

### Do not
Do not advise caller to enter traffic or move casualties without immediate danger. Do not let crash location remain vague; road direction is critical.
