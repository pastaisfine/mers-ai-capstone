## MED-007-SEIZURE - Seizure / Convulsion

**Category:** Medical  
**Priority:** P2 if ongoing/repeated/first seizure/injury/pregnancy/child; P1 if not breathing after seizure  
**Source basis:** IFRC first aid, seizure first aid sources in seed list  
**Retrieval tags:** seizure, fit, convulsion, jerking, foaming, epilepsy

### Dispatcher objective
Protect from injury, time the seizure, avoid harmful restraint, dispatch if risk factors present, monitor breathing after seizure.

### Critical questions
- Exact location?
- Is the seizure happening now?
- When did it start?
- Is the person breathing?
- Any injury, pregnancy, diabetes, first seizure, repeated seizure, water involved or child?
- Known epilepsy?

### Caller response script
Say: "Move objects away so they do not hit anything. Do not hold them down. Do not put anything in their mouth." Ask caller to note the time. After movement stops: "Check if they are breathing normally. Let them rest on their side if safe and keep their airway clear. Stay with them."

If seizure continues beyond local emergency threshold, repeats, or breathing is abnormal afterward, treat as high priority. If not breathing normally after seizure, switch to cardiac arrest protocol.

### Responder guidance
Ambulance when ongoing, repeated, first seizure, injury, pregnancy, diabetes, child, water-related event, prolonged postictal confusion or abnormal breathing. Handoff: start time, duration, breathing after seizure, injuries, known epilepsy/medication and safety hazards.

### Do not
Do not advise putting objects in the mouth. Do not restrain. Do not assume all seizures are harmless.

### RAG retrieval note
Retrieve this SOP when the transcript or classifier detects the trigger phrases above, a matching chief complaint, or a priority-changing life threat. If multiple protocols match, prefer the one with the immediate life threat and attach secondary SOPs as supporting context.
