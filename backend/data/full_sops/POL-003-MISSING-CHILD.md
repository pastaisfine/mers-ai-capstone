## POL-003-MISSING-CHILD - Missing Child / Lost Person High Risk

**Category:** Police/Search/Medical as needed  
**Priority:** P2/P1 if immediate danger, very young child, water/traffic, abduction suspicion or vulnerable person  
**Source basis:** Police dispatch lost-person essentials, child safeguarding principles  
**Retrieval tags:** missing child, lost kid, cannot find my son, abduction, wandered off, autism missing

### Dispatcher objective
Capture time-sensitive description, last known location, risk factors and search direction quickly for police response.

### Critical questions
- Child's name and age?
- Last seen exact location and time?
- What were they wearing?
- Direction of travel?
- Any medical/developmental condition?
- Nearby water, road, construction, forest?
- Any suspect/vehicle?
- Who is searching now?

### Caller response script
Say: "We will gather details quickly so responders know who to look for." Keep caregiver at last-known location if safe and useful. Ask for recent photo availability but do not delay reporting. If abduction or immediate danger is suspected, keep caller on line and gather suspect/vehicle details.

### Responder guidance
Dispatch police. Handoff: name, age, description, clothing, last-seen time/place, direction, vulnerabilities, hazards nearby, suspect/vehicle, caregiver phone and photo availability. If large area/risk, escalate search/rescue resources.

### Do not
Do not tell caregiver to wait 24 hours. Do not delay police notification for young/vulnerable missing persons.

### RAG retrieval note
Retrieve this SOP when the transcript or classifier detects the trigger phrases above, a matching chief complaint, or a priority-changing life threat. If multiple protocols match, prefer the one with the immediate life threat and attach secondary SOPs as supporting context.
