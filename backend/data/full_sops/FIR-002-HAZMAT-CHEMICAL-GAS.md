## FIR-002-HAZMAT-CHEMICAL-GAS - Hazardous Material / Chemical Spill / Gas Leak

**Category:** Fire/Hazmat/Medical/Police  
**Priority:** P1/P2 depending on exposure and spread  
**Source basis:** NADMA disaster response, hazmat sources in seed list, IFRC first aid  
**Retrieval tags:** chemical spill, gas smell, chlorine, ammonia, pesticide, unknown fumes, hazmat, lab accident

### Dispatcher objective
Keep callers and responders out of contaminated area, identify substance from a distance, dispatch hazmat/fire and medical support.

### Critical questions
- Exact location?
- What substance if known?
- Is there a label/placard number visible from safe distance?
- How many exposed?
- Symptoms?
- Indoor or outdoor?
- Wind direction if outdoors?
- Any fire/explosion risk?

### Caller response script
Say: "Move away from the substance and fumes now if you can do so safely. Do not touch it. Do not smell it to check. Keep others away." If indoors and safe to leave: evacuate upwind/uphill if possible. If gas leak suspected: "Do not switch lights or electrical devices on or off. Do not smoke. Leave the area."

For chemical on skin/eyes, retrieve approved decontamination first-aid script. General safe principle: remove from exposure and use clean water flushing when appropriate, but exact instruction depends on substance and local protocol.

### Responder guidance
Dispatch Bomba/hazmat, ambulance and police for perimeter as needed. Handoff: substance/name/placard, location, number exposed, symptoms, wind/indoor/outdoor, fire/explosion risk, evacuation status and safe staging location. Emphasize responder safety and need for PPE.

### Do not
Do not send caller back for labels. Do not let responders approach without hazmat awareness. Do not advise mixing chemicals or neutralizing substances.
