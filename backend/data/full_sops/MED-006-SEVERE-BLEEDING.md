## MED-006-SEVERE-BLEEDING - Severe Bleeding / Traumatic Hemorrhage

**Category:** Medical/Trauma  
**Priority:** P1 if uncontrolled or life-threatening; P2 otherwise  
**Source basis:** IFRC first aid, TLSM trauma framing, field triage principles  
**Retrieval tags:** bleeding a lot, blood pumping, amputation, deep cut, stab wound, gunshot, road accident bleeding

### Dispatcher objective
Control external bleeding with direct pressure, keep caller safe, dispatch ambulance and police if violence involved.

### Critical questions
- Exact location?
- Is the scene safe?
- Where is the bleeding?
- Is blood spurting or soaking through cloth?
- Is the patient awake and breathing normally?
- What caused the injury?
- Any weapon or attacker still present?

### Caller response script
Say: "Help is being arranged. Do not put yourself in danger. If it is safe, press firmly on the bleeding area now." Instruct: "Use a clean cloth, towel or clothing. Press hard and do not keep lifting it to check. If blood soaks through, put another cloth on top and keep pressing."

If a limb is severely bleeding and direct pressure is not working, local medical director should define whether dispatcher may instruct tourniquet use. For the prototype, the AI should retrieve the agency-approved bleeding-control script and escalate to human dispatcher if no approved script exists.

Tell caller: "Keep the person still and warm. Do not give food or drink. Tell me if they become sleepy, confused or stop breathing normally."

### Responder guidance
Ambulance. Add police if assault, stabbing, gunshot or unsafe scene. Handoff: bleeding site, severity, direct pressure started, consciousness, breathing, mechanism, weapon/suspect status, number of victims and access route. If industrial injury, include machinery/power isolation status.

### Do not
Do not tell caller to remove embedded objects. Do not ask caller to chase suspect. Do not minimize bleeding based on caller uncertainty.
