## POL-001-DOMESTIC-VIOLENCE - Domestic Violence / Family Violence in Progress

**Category:** Police/Medical/Social Support  
**Priority:** P1/P2 depending on immediate danger, weapon, injuries and suspect presence  
**Source basis:** WAO domestic violence toolkit, MOH One Stop Crisis Centre seed source, police safety principles  
**Retrieval tags:** husband hitting, wife assaulted, family violence, locked in, threatened, protection order, children in danger

### Dispatcher objective
Prioritize caller safety, use discreet communication if abuser nearby, dispatch police/medical, preserve dignity and avoid actions that increase danger.

### Critical questions
- Exact location?
- Are you safe to talk?
- Is the person hurting you there now?
- Any weapon?
- Any injuries or bleeding?
- Are children/elderly present?
- Can you move to a safer room/exit?
- Can you answer yes/no only?

### Caller response script
If safe to speak: "You are not in trouble. Help is being arranged. Tell me where you are." If abuser is nearby: "You can answer yes or no. Are you in danger now?" Do not ask long questions that expose the caller. If the caller cannot talk, use silent-call workflow and location metadata.

If immediate danger: "If you can leave safely, go to a public or safer place now. Do not confront the person. If you cannot leave, move away from weapons and rooms like the kitchen if safe." If injured, retrieve bleeding/medical protocol. Keep line open only if it does not increase risk.

### Responder guidance
Dispatch police urgently; add ambulance for injuries, pregnancy, strangulation, unconsciousness or severe distress. Handoff: suspect present/left, weapon, injuries, children present, caller's safe communication constraints, access issues, protection order if mentioned and need for discreet approach.

### Do not
Do not tell caller to confront, negotiate with or physically resist attacker unless immediate self-protection is unavoidable. Do not reveal shelter or sensitive details aloud if unsafe.
