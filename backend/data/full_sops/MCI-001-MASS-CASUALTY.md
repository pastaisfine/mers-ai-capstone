## MCI-001-MASS-CASUALTY - Mass Casualty Incident / Major Incident Mode

**Category:** Multi-agency  
**Priority:** MCI_MODE  
**Source basis:** NADMA disaster response, triage and emergency coordination principles  
**Retrieval tags:** many injured, bus crash, explosion, building collapse, train crash, mass poisoning, multiple emergency calls same location

### Dispatcher objective
Stop single-patient workflow, establish incident scale, hazards, staging, command, casualty categories and agency coordination.

### Critical questions
- Exact incident location and access?
- What happened?
- Approximate number injured?
- Any fire, smoke, chemicals, weapons or structural danger?
- How many not moving/not breathing?
- How many seriously bleeding/trapped?
- Safe staging area?
- Who is caller and where are they?

### Caller response script
Say: "Help is being arranged for a major incident. Do not put yourself in danger." Ask caller to move to a safe vantage point if possible. Gather counts by observable severity: not moving, trapped, bleeding heavily, walking wounded. Do not ask caller to perform formal triage unless trained and safe.

If caller can help safely: "Tell people who can walk to move to a safe area away from danger. Stay away from smoke, fuel, unstable structures or traffic." Retrieve specific life-saving protocols only for the caller's immediate patient if safe.

### Responder guidance
Activate MCI/major incident workflow. Dispatch multi-agency response, command officer, ambulance surge, Bomba/rescue, police traffic/security, hospital notification and staging. Handoff: incident type, casualty estimate, hazards, access/staging, number trapped, critical observable victims and caller vantage point. Maintain common operating picture with updates.

### Do not
Do not overload one dispatcher with all tasks if team resources exist. Do not send responders into unsecured hazards. Do not allow the AI to assign definitive triage tags without trained responder input.

### RAG retrieval note
Retrieve this SOP when the transcript or classifier detects the trigger phrases above, a matching chief complaint, or a priority-changing life threat. If multiple protocols match, prefer the one with the immediate life threat and attach secondary SOPs as supporting context.
