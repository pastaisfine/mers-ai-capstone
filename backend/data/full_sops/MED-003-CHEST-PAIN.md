## MED-003-CHEST-PAIN - Chest Pain / Suspected Heart Attack

**Category:** Medical  
**Priority:** P2_EMERGENCY; P1 if unconscious or not breathing  
**Source basis:** MOH emergency care framing, IFRC medical conditions  
**Retrieval tags:** chest pain, pressure, tightness, pain to arm/jaw/back, sweating, nausea, heart attack

### Dispatcher objective
Recognize time-sensitive possible cardiac event, reduce exertion, dispatch ambulance and monitor for arrest.

### Critical questions
- Exact location?
- Age and sex?
- Is the patient awake and breathing normally?
- When did pain start?
- Where is the pain and does it spread?
- Any sweating, nausea, shortness of breath or fainting?
- Any heart history?
- Is the patient alone?

### Caller response script
Say: "Help is being arranged. Have the patient stop all activity and sit or lie in the most comfortable position. Do not let them drive themselves." Keep the caller on the line. Ask them to unlock the door, put pets away and gather medication list if this does not delay care.

Tell caller: "If the patient becomes unconscious or stops breathing normally, tell me immediately." If that happens, switch to suspected cardiac arrest protocol. Avoid lengthy discussion of diagnosis. The caller only needs to know that chest pain can be serious and responders are needed.

### Responder guidance
Ambulance response. Handoff: onset time, pain description, radiation, breathing status, consciousness, cardiac history, medication/allergy if available, and whether patient has collapsed. Recommend hospital pre-alert according to local chest-pain pathway if applicable.

### Do not
Do not advise private transport for ongoing severe chest pain. Do not diagnose heart attack with certainty. Do not recommend aspirin unless local medical control specifically approves that dispatcher script.
